extending the circuit

Your small business is a circuit too.

Most small-business forms are feeders into the personal-return circuit — a Schedule C net or a K-1 that becomes an input node on your 1040 — with a few new feedback loops. Here is the same idea applied: a self-employed circuit, and the S-corp reasonable-compensation optimization, both runnable.

Not tax advice. An educational model with simplified 2025 assumptions (non-SSTB, sole prop has no W-2 wages/UBIA, the Form 8995-A wage limit and §199A phase-in approximated). Figures are illustrative; reasonable compensation is a facts-and-circumstances legal standard. Consult a professional.

The form map

The ecosystem forks on entity type. Three of the four are pass-throughs whose output lands on your 1040; only the C-corp is its own taxpayer.

Self-employed circuit (Schedule C)

Net profit flows to Schedule 1 → 1040 line 9; self-employment tax bolts on through Schedule SE → Schedule 2; the adjustments (½ SE tax, the SEP/Solo-401(k) contribution, SE-health) lower AGI; the QBI deduction lowers taxable income. The SEP is the retirement fixed point (Pub 560’s 25%→20% is the closed-form resolution).

S-corp reasonable-compensation optimizer

An S-corp owner splits the profit into W-2 reasonable compensation (subject to payroll tax) and a distribution (not). Lower comp saves payroll tax — but above the QBI threshold the deduction is capped at 50% of W-2 wages, so comp that’s too low chokes the QBI deduction. The optimizer sweeps comp to minimize total federal tax (income tax + both FICA halves), respecting your reasonable-comp floor.

Sources & assumptions

  • Schedule C / Schedule SE; Form 8995 / 8995-A (QBI, §199A) with the 50%-wages limit and the threshold phase-in; Pub. 560 (self-employed retirement, the 25%→20% rate); Form 7206 / Pub. 974 (SE-health ↔ PTC).
  • 2025 constants: SS wage base $176,100; FICA 12.4% OASDI + 2.9% Medicare; QBI threshold $197,300 single / $394,600 MFJ with a $50k/$100k phase-in; defined-contribution limit $70,000.
  • Simplifications: non-SSTB; sole proprietor modeled with no W-2 wages and no qualified property (UBIA = 0), so QBI is wage-limited above the threshold; reasonable compensation is exogenous (a legal standard, not optimized to a floor the IRS would accept); additional Medicare, NIIT, and state tax are omitted.
